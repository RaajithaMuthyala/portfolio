Biomedical Data Analytics

Forecasting and Mapping Maternal Health Trends with DHIS2 & R

Project Overview

This project leveraged biomedical datasets and health system data (DHIS2) to analyze and forecast reproductive health trends in Sierra Leone. The focus was on malnutrition, birth rate prediction, and antenatal coverage. Our approach combined geospatial mapping, statistical modeling, and qualitative insights to support public health decision-making.

Key Features

• Time series forecasting of maternal and child health indicators using ARIMA and polynomial regression.

• Geospatial mapping of ANC2 coverage by chiefdom, with focus on forest reserve proximities.

• Visual dashboards created in DHIS2 for real-time policy insights.

• Team-level NLP sentiment analysis to assess interpretation alignment across domains.

Technologies Used

Analytics: R (forecast, ggplot2), DHIS2, Python (NLP)

Data Format: XML-based health indicators, structured .csv exports

Geospatial Tools: Custom shapefile overlays, QGIS (for validation)

Dashboards: DHIS2 Tracker + custom visual components

Impact & Insights

The project revealed underreporting patterns in rural regions near forest reserves. Forecasts for malnutrition and ANC2 coverage guided targeted interventions. Stakeholder recommendations supported increased resource allocation and targeted outreach programs in underserved regions. Our visualizations provided intuitive narratives for policy action.
