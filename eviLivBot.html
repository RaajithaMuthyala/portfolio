EviLivBot

Explainable AI for Liver Disease and SDOH

Project Overview

EviLivBot is a domain-specific, explainable AI application designed to analyze biomedical literature related to liver disease and Social Determinants of Health (SDOH). It fine-tunes the LLaMA 3.1 8B model to extract relevant biomedical entities and provide evidence-backed responses through a user-friendly interface.

Key Features

Fine-tuning of the LLaMA 3.1 8B model on liver disease and SDOH abstracts.

Biomedical Named Entity Recognition (NER) to extract meaningful clinical concepts.

Explainable AI pipeline with evidence retrieval from structured JSON data.

Streamlit-based web interface for interactive exploration.

Technologies Used

LLM: LLaMA 3.1 8B (fine-tuned using PEFT/LoRA)

Libraries: Hugging Face Transformers, SpaCy, SciBERT, SentenceTransformers

Interface: Streamlit (for frontend + interaction)

Data: PubMed abstracts, MIMIC clinical notes, MedQA/SDOH concepts

Explainability & Evidence Retrieval

EviLivBot links extracted entities with evidence passages and source DOI links. This enables trust and interpretability, especially in sensitive domains like healthcare.

Streamlit UI

The frontend enables users to paste clinical text, view extracted entities (e.g., diseases, treatments, risk factors), and click on them to explore related research excerpts.
